CalCentralCalendar
Web Development

CalCentralCalendar

Description:
Currently, there's no easy way to take our class schedule from Berkeley's scheduling system and input it into our Google Calendar schedule. This project allows users to get a Google Chrome Extension that will enter your Berkeley schedule into Google Calendar with just 2 clicks. It uses the Chrome Extension to get the schedule from the CalCentral Shopping Cart page and then uses the Google Calendar API to create and populate events obtained from the class schedule data from CalCentral.

Impact:
This project significantly cuts down the amount of time spent at the beginning of the semester trying to enter your Berkeley schedule into your personal Google Calendar.

Securing NFC Communication
Mobile/Research

Securing NFC Communication using In-Built Sensors

Description:
The paper/project was about using built-in android sensors to map physical actions for NFC security. I used a Samsung Galaxy S7 and the various sensors that came with it to map out various physical actions such as flipping the phone, rotating the phone, and other more complex actions. I conducted this research under the guidance of Prof. Sriram Chellappan at USF. The goal was to enhanced mobile security via an extra layer of sensor-authentication, using data from smartphone sensors and NFC data authentication for secure file sharing algorithm.

Impact:
The proof of concept was shown; we were able to successfully able to detect and map various physical actions and then establish NFC communication. Further use of the research was used in USF's Green Building Initiative for monitoring indoor settings (temperature/pressure/light levels) by facility managers.

Using:
  • Android Studio
  • USF Research Lab
